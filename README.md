### Overview

**Playtime:  1.5 - 2 hours**

To win the LDC (LEGO Dungeon Crawler) you must find the key to the boss room. This key can be found by looting a chest after defeating a monster. If no player finds the key, but all rooms are cleared, then the boss room will appear unlocked. 
Once the boss is vanquished, the nearest wall crumbles, allowing players to exit. All other players have one remaining turn to leave the dungeon, otherwise the room collapses and the exit shuts forever. Players who leave the dungeon keep their character/minifig and are allowed to use it in any other dungeon.

    
The instructions are LONG but worthwhile to read, and ½ the pages are guides used throughout the game. The LDC was designed to grow and evolve as you play. Since player characters can persist beyond one dungeon, a history begins to unfold between you and the other players. This causes great change to The LDC, and makes each dungeon interaction unique. 
This game is built off of LEGO as a creative backbone, and is the tool that brings The LDC to life. The beauty of the dungeon is that the instructions apply to any of your LEGOs, so feel free to stretch your creative muscles and get use out of those dusty figures! Players can decide for themselves what is considered a Boss, Monster, Magic Weapon, Light armor etc. Set a theme for your dungeon or completely let loose!
>- Classic Medieval Dungeon: Knights/undead as a horde, dragons for bosses, direwolves for Beasts
>- Minecraft Dungeon: skeletons and zombies for hordes, Ravager beasts, wither boss
>- Star Wars Dungeon:  Clone trooper / Droid hordes, AT ST or Jedi for a boss.

## INSTRUCTIONS
### Game Set-up
LEGO Crawler Dungeons is a DnD lite board game, with a board composed of modular rooms that link together to create a maze-like grid. The Crawler is balanced for 3-5 people with a standard of 12 rooms per dungeon. It is optimally played with 4 players.  Playing with fewer players is for those who want a PvE challenge. Playing with more players adds more chaos, and more player interaction However games can be played with as many, or few players as you want.

Orient and align the rooms, then connect together using pins. Give each room a number, 1 - 12. Add the starting platforms to any room at the edge of the dungeon with a doorway that exits the dungeon. Starting platforms cannot be attached to a room adjacent to another room with a starting platform. 

Choose a Merchant Room by rolling a d12 (for 12 rooms) and placing a market stall and travelling merchant inside the room you roll.

Finally, place the watchtowers. By default, there should be 1 tower for every 2 players. If any of the players are bannered, add the same number of towers as the highest bannered players level. 

    Ex.) A dungeon with a level 4 bannered player would have 4 towers
Towers are placed next to “Bridges”, in between 2 rooms; There are always 2 rooms adjacent to a tower. Towers cannot be placed between rooms with starting platforms.

## GAMEPLAY
#### Turn Phase
A player may complete one main action per turn and as many light actions as they want. This can be done in any combination. Watchtower actions are a special type of light action. These can be performed only once a turn, but have no effect on your ability to perform an additional main action (Ex. A player can Light A Signal Fire and Explore a new room in the same turn).

### Light Actions
1. Invade a players room (Anytime, including other players' turns)
    1. If a player is in the combat phase, after every exchange between combatants, players in adjacent connecting rooms may roll to enter the room of the player in combat. If they roll even, they may enter. 
    1. Once the invader enters, they must state whether they are fighting with or against the current players in the room.
1. Attacking a player
    1. If you are in the same room as another player, you can choose to attack the player. If a combat-phase has not already begun, start it, and the attacking player will roll first.
    1. Upon killing a player, the victim will drop their minifigures head. If this head is taken out of the dungeon, it can be exchanged at the Gildhall for 3VP.
- Heal / Eat food (Your turn, anytime)
    - Food heals 15HP
    - Potions Heal 35HP
- One-shot items  (Anytime, including other players' turns)
- Equipping armor / armaments - (Your turn, anytime)
- Loot the room (Only able to be done after delivering the final strike to a monster)
-   Refer to the monster guide to determine the number of loot rolls you receive. See loot guide
- Extinguish Watchtower (Your turn, anytime IF in a room connected to a lit watchtower)
    - Climb the tower to extinguish the flame. This slows down bannered players, and allows you to gild armor at towers once again.
- Shop (Only able to be done if player is in a room with a Travelling Merchant)
    - Exchange VP for goods, see Store Inventory

###  Watchtower Actions  
> All watchtower actions must occur at the beginning of a player's turn. The player must be in a room with open access to a hallway with a watchtower. At the beginning of their turn, before any main action, a player can either:

- Light A Signal Fire (Bannered Only) 
    - To light the fire players have two options; they can kill a player ina  room adjacent to the tower, automatically lighting the fire. Or, you can place their banner at the top of the tower. Both rooms adjacent to the tower are now “Branded”. Enter the room and summon a Gilded Templar. If defeated, loot a branded chest, reclaim your banner, and light the signal fire.
- Gild An Armor Piece 
    - Gamble/risk a piece of armor by placing it at the top of the tower. If a watchtower is lit, you cannot gild. This marks the two rooms adjacent to the tower as “Gilded”. Enter a gilded room to summon a Gilded Monster. Upon defeat, loot a random (style) Full Plate armor piece. Keep in mind that helmets upgrade to helmets, and chestplate to chestplates. If a player dies before gilding their gear, their armor and the monster remains for other players.

### Main Actions (1 Per Turn)
- Rest
	- Stay in the same position and for no cost heal 50% of your total health back.
	    >Ex.) HP: 13/50, rest -> HP: 38/50
	    >
        >Ex.) HP: 29/50, rest -> HP: 50/50
- Move
    - Move your character through opened rooms (maximum of 2 per turn). These rooms must have an open hallway connecting them	
- Explore
    - Knock down the door of an adjacent room connected to your current room. Place your fig into the new room and summon the monster. Refer to the Monster Summoning Guide to summon, then you may begin the combat phase.
- Combat Phase
    - Singleplayer combat phase
        - Another player will roll for the monster. Roll for STL to attack first; Roll the dice for your combat power (See equipment section), deal that damage to the monster's health. Now the monster will roll to attack (Monster roll outlined in monster section). The player will  take the damage that the monster rolls. The player can choose to roll DEX and flee instead of attacking. Otherwise continue fighting until the monster or player dies.
        - If the player dies, they will drop all of their items in the room, remove their minifig, and leave the monster. The monster’s health and level shall remain for the others.
        - If the monster dies, the player will roll for loot
    - Multiplayer combat phase
        - The player to first enter the room will attack first, then each player follows counter clockwise. Each player will select their target before attacking, monster or player. Monsters roll after all the players, the rolled damage applied to all players in the room.
        - The player to deliver the killing blow to the monster will receive its rewards
    - Combat continues until all monsters are vanquished, and either players cease combat, or one player remains in the room 


### Boss Fight
The boss fight is what allows players to win/complete the dungeon. The boss is summoned in one of two ways; a player loots the dungeon key, or all rooms in the dungeon are cleared. 

The player to loot the key / deliver the final strike to the last remaining monster will roll a die (Standard dungeon is 12 rooms, use 2 D6) to choose the boss room. The chosen room MUST be one of the rooms that is not explored. If an explored room is rolled, roll again. The room will remain locked until the player with the key opens it.

If the dungeon is vanquished, the last player to fight will roll a die to choose the boss room. If a room with a player in it is rolled, roll again. The boss room should already be unlocked, if not remove any door pieces.

Once the room is chosen, hoist the banners to make it easily visible, then race to battle the boss! When a player enters the room, roll a d20 to determine the boss type. All bosses have the same base set up: 650HP, 100CP. During the combat phase, the boss will deal splash damage to all targets in the room. Each boss type has certain powers, or debuffs/buffs depending on its type, as follows:
- (Roll 1 - 5) - Poisoner
    - Deals 5 guaranteed poison damage per turn. Every time the boss damages for >= 70CP, poison damage doubles.
- (Roll 6 - 10) - Tank
    - HP 1000 CP 75
- (Roll 11 - 15) - Summoner
    - Every 2 turns, summon 3 additional undead companions (lvl 1)
- (Roll 16 - 20) -  Glass Canon
    - HP 500, CP 200

Upon defeating the boss, the player delivering the final strike has a choice; if they are a human, they may choose to fuse their soul to the monster, becoming a shadowed spirit binder. If you choose to bind your spirit you will not be awarded VP (Victory points awarded to players who leave a defeated dungeon).

Afterwards, remove a wall piece in the boss room, or a wall piece in the closest adjacent room. This will be the dungeon exit. All remaining players have 1 final turn to move to that room and escape the dungeon. If you do not exit that turn, the room collapses, and all remaining players inside the dungeon die.

If a player exits the dungeon they become a bannered player, level up, and are awarded the choice of either 1 additional inventory slot, or 1 permanent CP buff (+20CP) every 2 levels. Additionally, if you did any damage to the boss, you are given 7VPs. If you dealt the killing blow, you gain an additional 2VP.

###  Gild Hall Guide
The Gild Hall acts as the central force of the dungeon world. Upon defeating strong foe, bosses, and even other players, they will award you with Victory points. VP is a currency that can be spent at both the Gild Hall Market and the Travelling Merchant.
	
The travelling merchant spawns during the creation of the dungeon, and can be used as a “safe tile”. No monster can be summoned here, and no combat can take place. You can choose to rest here, or trade with the travelling merchant.

As listed throughout the instructions, all of the ways to earn VP, and the corresponding amount is as follows: (Note that bannered players receive +1VP for each player / boss killed per level; level 2 +2VP, level 6 +6VP…)

1. Helping defeat a boss  +7VP
1. Bringing a wanted players head to the Gild Hall +6VP
1. Bringing a players head to the Gild Hall +3VP
1. Using an Enchanted Book’s chaos output to revive a player when no players are currently dead +3VP
1. Delivering the killing blow to a boss +2VP
1. Selling items 1-2VP
1. Killing any level 3 monster +1VP

#### Gild Hall Shop:

1. SELL 1 lvl 2 item for 1 VP
1. SELL 1 lvl 3 item for 2 VP
1. 1VP for 4 food OR 2 Health pots

1. 6VP to enchant any combat item (+1 d20 roll per attack)

    - This player receives the benefits from the Gildhall, and levels up, but they do not keep any previous gear. They may roll for starter loot at the beginning of the next dungeon.
1. 8VP: Choose any level 3 Weapon / Armor (No full plate armor)
1. 9VP: Make weapon unique (Add a unique modifier / power to your weapon)

## Monster Summoning
To summon a normal monster, first roll D20 to determine type (listed below), followed by a second stat scaler roll that will determine the monsters level: roll 1-12 (lvl 1), roll 13-17 (lvl 2), roll 18-20 (lvl 3)

To summon a Gilded Templar, simply roll for stat scaler to determine its level. There are no templar variants, and therefore there is no type roll.  Templars are stronger variants of Invaders, and will use the same base CP, with an additional 75HP to the base. Every 2 combat turns, if a templar is not killed, they will summon two remnants to defend them (Same HP / CP as horde members)
	
Bannered players summon normal monsters with additional buffs to scale to their power: All summoned monsters start with base health and additional 50 HP per level. (level 1 +50HP to all, level 2 +100HP to all…) Bannered players' stat scaler AND summoning rolls are increased by 2 every level. (level 1 stat scaler is +2 to all rolls, level 2 is +4 to all rolls…) Any Level 3 monster awards 1VP!
### Monster Types
- (Roll 1 - 6) Invader: a minifig invader! (Knight, ninja, viking etc.)
    - Critical hit: "Pierce" - inflicts bleed damage, take an additional 5 damage per turn until combat ends
    - Drops: 1x loot roll | 2x loot rolls | 3x loot rolls
    - HP: 90 | 110 | 130
    - CP: D20+D10 |  2xD20 | 3XD20
- (Roll 7 - 11) Beasts: any realistically scaled animal minifig (Bears, wolves, Elephants etc.)
    - Critical hit: "Chomp" - Knaws a chunk out of you to regain health, heal 50% of damage dealt
    - Drops: 1x loot roll | 2x loot rolls | 3x loot rolls
    - HP: 105 | 130 | 165
    - CP: D20 + D10 | 2xD20 |3xD20
- (Roll 12 - 14) Horde: multiple similar/identical minifigs
    - Note: Lvl 1 horde summons 4 members, level 2 summons 6, level 3 summons 8
    -Critical hit: "Reinforcements" -  Does no additional damage, instead 2 reinforcements arrive
    - Drops: 1x loot roll / 2 horde kills
    - HP: Each Horde member has 25 HP
    - CP: D12 per member alive
- (Roll 16 - 18) Mounted Rider: Invaders riding on mounted creatures (horses, wolves, and dinosaurs)
    - Note: Mount and Rider have two separate HP. Damage is reduced by ½ when either dies.
    - Critical Hit: "Trample" - Tramples you and is able to attack two times
    - Drops: 2x loot rolls | 3x loot rolls | 4x loot rolls
    - Invader HP: 90 | 110 | 130
    - Mount HP:   75 | 80 | 95
    - CP: 3xD20 | 4xD20 | 5xD20
- (Roll 19, 20) Apostle: Large humanoid or intellectual creatures (giants, trolls, wyrms, drakes, and smaller dragons)
    - Critical Hit: "Divine Strike" -  Multiply current attack by 2
    - Drops: 3x loot rolls | 4x loot rolls | 5x loot rolls

## Race / Class Guide
Race and class will determine your playstyle for The LDC. Classes are a way to fine tune your playstyle; do you like magic? Maybe you want to use a big sword? Keep things light with some knives? Your class will give buffs to certain weapons or armor. 

Race impacts your playstyle by giving you powerful unique abilities. Unlike class, your race should closely resemble the minifig you create

>- Undead: Skeletons, zombies etc.
>- Beastman: Animal / wolf like minifigs
>- Afflicted: Sickly / alien like minifigs
>- Sinnick: Demon / vampiric minifigs
>- Human: Any normal humanoid minifig
>- Dwarf: Minifigs with short legs

## One Shot Items
One shot items are held in a player's inventory and can only be used one time. After use, they are returned to the “store” (The store is where all OSI’s are held, feel free to make this an external build to the dungeon. The store has a specific inventory listed below).
### Item Guide
1. (Roll 1 - 4) Map - Teleport to a random room on the floor
1. (Roll 5 - 7) Raw Gemstone - TP to random player (All other players roll d20, teleport to the highest roller)
1. (Roll 8 - 10) Spell Book - Roll d20 for chaos output (See below)
1. (Roll 11 / 12) Dynamite - Roll d20, multiply by 4 and deal that damage to all other players and monsters in the room
1. (Roll 13 / 14) Shovel - dig into an adjacent room, even if there is not path
1. (Roll 15 / 16) Polished Gemstone - TP to another player
1. (Roll 17 / 18) Sage Frog - Summon another version of yourself to aid in combat
1. (Roll 19 / 20) Blight Bone - Revives a monster in the current or an adjacent room to the player, regardless of pathways 

### Chaos Output
Use a spell book to let chaos take odds into its own hands
1. (Roll 1 - 4) Food ‘O Plenty! 8 food spawns on the ground of your room
1. (Roll 5) Gear Swap! All players swap all equipped  gear with the player to the right.
1. (Roll 6) Bear trap - Roll a d12 to plant a bear trap in a room. The first player to enter the room is trapped and cannot move until rescued by another player. Take 5 damage every turn until saved or death
1. (Roll 7 - 11) All players swap positions with the person to their left
1. (Roll 12- 14) Teleport all players back to their starting positions
1. (Roll 15) Everyone rolls d20, the lowest roll is marked as wanted. Their head value is now 6VP
1. (Roll 16 - 17) Choose a player who will remove all their equipped gear and place it at the start. (Their figure remains)
1. (Roll 18) Charmed, all other players roll a d20, highest roller charms you! You have fallen in love with another player! You cannot leave the dungeon without them.
1.  (Roll 19) The closest player (to book user) has to change their race!
1. (Roll 20) Revive a player. Choose a player to revive, the player will respawn wherever their head is. (If in another player's inventory, spawn in the same room as the player. After respawning you may roll once for loot. If no one is dead, the book turns into 3VP
